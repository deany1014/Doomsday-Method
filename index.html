What is the Doomsday Algorithm?

Invented by mathematician John Conway, Doomsday lets you mentally calculate the day of the week for any date in history. Every year has an anchor day called its Doomsday — and certain easy-to-remember dates always fall on that day. Once you know the anchor, finding any date is simple arithmetic.

Step 1 — Find the Century Anchor

1

Look up the century

Each century repeats on a cycle of 4. The anchor days for the four possible centuries are:


Century (1900s, 2000s…)Anchor DayCode
1800sFriday5
1900sWednesday3
2000sTuesday2
2100sSunday0

Example: for any date in the 2000s, the century anchor is 2 (Tuesday).

Step 2 — Find the Year Anchor

2

Calculate with the last two digits (call them y)

a = (y/12) + (y mod 12) + ((y mod 12)/4)

Each part is floor divided (throw away the remainder).


Example — year 2026, so y = 26:


PartCalculationResult
⌊26 / 12⌋How many times does 12 fit?2
26 mod 12Remainder2
⌊2 / 4⌋Leap correction0
Sum2 + 2 + 04

Add the century anchor: 2 + 4 = 6. Take mod 7: 6 mod 7 = 6 → Saturday. That's the Doomsday for 2026.

Step 4 — Count to the Target Date

4

Find the nearest Doomsday, then count

Find the closest Doomsday date in the same month. Then count forward or backward, adding/subtracting days mod 7.

Year Formula

Doomsday = (century_anchor + ⌊y/12⌋ + (y mod 12) + ⌊(y mod 12)/4⌋) mod 7

where y = last two digits of year
